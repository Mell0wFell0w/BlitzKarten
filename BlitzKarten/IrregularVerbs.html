German Lesson: Irregular Verbs

In this lesson, we'll cover German irregular verbs, or unregelmäßige Verben. Unlike regular verbs, irregular verbs don't follow standard conjugation patterns, and their forms change depending on tense and subject. Understanding these is crucial as they're common in everyday language.

Characteristics of German Irregular Verbs

Irregular verbs in German often have vowel changes in their root when conjugated, especially in the second and third person singular forms (e.g., du, er/sie/es). Here are some examples:

Infinitive 2nd Person Singular (du) 3rd Person Singular (er/sie/es) English Translation
sehen siehst sieht to see
lesen liest liest to read
fahren fährst fährt to drive
laufen läufst läuft to run

Conjugation of Common Irregular Verbs

Verb: Sein (To Be)

Sein is one of the most common irregular verbs in German, used to describe states of being or identity:

Pronoun Conjugation
Ich (I) bin
Du (You) bist
Er/Sie/Es (He/She/It) ist
Wir (We) sind
Ihr (You all) seid
Sie (They) sind

Verb: Haben (To Have)

The verb haben is also irregular and is frequently used in everyday conversation.

Pronoun Conjugation
Ich (I) habe
Du (You) hast
Er/Sie/Es (He/She/It) hat
Wir (We) haben
Ihr (You all) habt
Sie (They) haben

Practice

Now, try to create sentences using these verbs:

  • Ich bin glücklich. (I am happy.)
  • Er sieht den Hund. (He sees the dog.)
  • Wir haben ein Auto. (We have a car.)

Remember to practice these verbs regularly, as they form the basis of many common expressions in German!
